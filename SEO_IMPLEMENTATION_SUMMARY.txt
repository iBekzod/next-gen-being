CONTENT STRATEGY (Month 1+)
================================================================================

PUBLISHING FREQUENCY:
- Target: 2-3 posts per week minimum for growth
- 1-2 posts/week: Maintain current position
- 4+ posts/week: Burnout risk without team

CONTENT MIX:
- 40% Evergreen (tutorials, guides, how-tos)
- 35% Trend analysis (what's happening in tech)
- 20% News/breaking updates
- 5% Personal insights (opinion, predictions)

CONTENT LENGTH TARGETS:
- Breaking news: 400-600 words (short, digestible)
- Quick tips: 600-800 words (skimmable)
- Tutorials: 1,500-2,000 words (comprehensive)
- In-depth guides: 2,000-2,500 words (authority)
- Comparisons: 1,800-2,200 words (multiple perspectives)

TITLE FORMULAS (Proven to rank):
1. How-To: "How to [Achieve] Without [Pain]"
   Example: "How to Learn AI Without Getting Overwhelmed"

2. Lists: "[Odd Number] [Noun] Every [Audience] Needs in 2025"
   Example: "7 AI Tools Every Developer Needs in 2025"

3. Questions: "What Is [Concept] and Why Does It Matter?"
   Example: "What Is Prompt Engineering and Why It Matters?"

4. Comparisons: "[A] vs [B]: Which [Outcome]?"
   Example: "ChatGPT vs Claude: Which AI Is Better for Code?"

5. Guides: "The Complete Guide to [Topic]"
   Example: "The Complete Guide to Prompt Engineering"

6. News: "[Company] [Action]: [Impact]"
   Example: "OpenAI Releases GPT-5: Here's What Changed"

META DESCRIPTIONS:
- Length: 135-160 characters (mobile doesn't truncate)
- Include: Primary keyword + benefit statement
- Format by type: See SEO_CONTENT_TEMPLATES.md for specific formulas

INTERNAL LINKING:
- Goal: 5-10 links per 2,000 word article
- Formula: 1 link per 200-300 words
- Anchor text: Use keyword variations (not "click here")
- Distribution: Spread throughout, not clustered
- Priority: Link to pillar pages + related clusters

================================================================================
IMPORTANT REMINDERS
================================================================================

1. Results take time: 3-6 months minimum
   - Google crawls and indexes new content slowly
   - Trust signals build over time
   - Don't give up too early

2. Quality over quantity
   - Better to publish 2 great posts than 5 mediocre ones
   - Google rewards expertise and originality
   - Focus on depth, not just length

3. E-E-A-T for tech content
   - Show your expertise and credentials
   - Share personal experience
   - Be transparent about limitations
   - Cite credible sources

4. Niche focus works better
   - Don't try to beat TechCrunch (101M backlinks)
   - Focus on specific angles (AI, startups, dev tools)
   - Build authority in your niche
   - Start with long-tail keywords

5. Consistency matters most
   - Maintain 2-3 posts/week even if slow start
   - Google rewards consistent publishers
   - Regular updates signal an active site
   - Content refresh boosts old posts

6. Monitor and iterate
   - Track what's working
   - Double down on winners
   - Learn from underperformers
   - Adjust strategy monthly
